What is a Church Without Curtains?

When Jesus died, the heavy curtain separating people from God was torn open. There are no more barriers. Today, it means we drop our fake perfection. We do not hide our messy, real lives behind a curtain of religious performance. We come to God exactly as we are.

How Algorithms Trap Us

Tech companies build apps to hijack a chemical in your brain called dopamine. It is your brain's craving chemical. They use unpredictable rewards to keep you scrolling. Over time, your brain gets exhausted. You cannot just use willpower to stop; you need your mind renewed.

Why We Hide

When life gets hard, we look for a safe place to hide. We use our screens as new curtains to escape:

  • Stress: We scroll to numb racing thoughts.
  • Pain: We shop to buy temporary comfort.
  • Failure: We game or stream to feel like we are winning.

Understanding Shame

Shame tells us, "If people knew the real me, they would not love me." Shame takes different forms:

  • Performance: Fear that you must look perfect online.
  • Avoidance: Guilt from ignoring real life for a screen.
  • Secret: Feeling dirty from hidden habits.

Healing Together

True freedom requires community and God's help.

The Mosaic

A mosaic is beautiful art made entirely of broken pieces. If you hide your broken pieces behind a curtain, you cannot be part of the picture. We need honest community to heal together without judgment.

A Clean Heart (Tahor)

Trying to fix your habits by just trying harder is like putting up a new curtain. We need God to step in. We ask Him for a Tahor heart—a completely clean, unmixed heart that desires Him more than our digital escapes.
